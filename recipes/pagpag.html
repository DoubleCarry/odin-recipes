Pagpag Recipe

Description

is a Filipino dish made from salvaged leftover food, often collected from fast food restaurants. The term "pagpag" means "to shake off" in Filipino, referring to the act of shaking off dirt or debris from the leftovers before cooking. Originally a survival food in impoverished communities, pagpag is now known as a symbol of resilience and resourcefulness in Filipino culture.

Ingidients

  • Leftover cooked meat (usually fried chicken or pork)
  • 2 tablespoons cooking oil
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 small onion, chopped
  • 1 medium tomato, chopped
  • 1 tablespoon soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon oyster sauce (optional)
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Cooked rice (for serving)

Directions

  1. Wash the leftover meat thoroughly to remove any dirt or debris.
  2. Cut the meat into small bite-sized pieces.
  3. Heat oil in a pan over medium heat.
  4. Sauté garlic until golden brown, then add onion and tomato. Cook until softened.
  5. Add the chopped leftover meat and stir-fry for 3–5 minutes.
  6. Pour in the soy sauce and oyster sauce. Mix well.
  7. Season with salt and pepper to taste.
  8. Cook for another 2–3 minutes, or until everything is heated through.
  9. Serve hot with cooked rice.
