Puto Pao Recipe

Description

A type of special puto with meat filling. I made it extra special by topping it with salted egg. This is easy to make. Your family will love it. How to Make It is easy and fun to make puto pao at home

Ingidients

  • Asado Filling:
    • 1 lb. pork, sliced into small pieces
    • 1 tsp Knorr Liquid Seasoning
    • 1 onion, minced
    • 3 Tbsp soy sauce
    • 2 pieces star anise
    • 1/4 cup brown sugar
    • 3 Tbsp cooking oil
    • 2 tsp cornstarch
    • Ground black pepper, to taste
  • Puto Batter:
    • 1 cup water
    • 2 cups all-purpose flour
    • 2 cups cake flour
    • 2 1/2 Tbsp baking powder
    • 1 1/2 cups sugar
    • 1/2 cup melted butter
    • 1 egg
    • 1 cup evaporated milk
    • 2 cups water

    • Directions

    1. Heat cooking oil in a pot and sauté the minced onion until translucent.
    2. Add the pork and cook until lightly browned.
    3. Pour in soy sauce and water, then bring to a boil.
    4. Add the star anise and cover; simmer on low heat for 30–35 minutes.
    5. Stir in the brown sugar, Knorr Liquid Seasoning, and ground black pepper.
    6. Mix cornstarch with 3 Tbsp water to make a slurry, pour into the pot, and cook until sauce thickens. Set aside the filling.
    7. In a large bowl, whisk together the all-purpose flour, cake flour, sugar, and baking powder.
    8. Add the egg, evaporated milk, 1 cup water, and melted butter; mix until smooth.
    9. Pour about 1 tablespoon of batter into each mold. Add 1–1.5 tsp of asado filling. Fill the mold up to 3/4 full with more batter.
    10. Arrange molds in a steamer and steam for 2–3 minutes.
    11. Top each with a slice of salted duck egg. Continue steaming until a total of 15 minutes is reached.
    12. Remove molds from steamer and let cool.
    13. Gently loosen the Puto Pao from the molds and transfer to a serving plate. Serve warm.
